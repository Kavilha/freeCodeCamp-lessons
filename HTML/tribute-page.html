Marie Curie

The Mother of Modern Physics

Here's a time line of Marie Curie's life:

    Early Life & Education

  • 1867 – Born Maria Skłodowska on November 7 in Warsaw, Poland (then part of the Russian Empire).
  • 1883-85 – Graduated with a gold medal from secondary school; worked as a governess to support her sister’s education.
  • 1891-94 – Moved to Paris, studied at the Sorbonne (University of Paris), earning degrees in physics and mathematics.

    • Scientific Discoveries & Nobel Prizes

  • 1895 – Married Pierre Curie, beginning a groundbreaking scientific partnership.
  • 1898 – Discovered polonium and radium with Pierre, coining the term "radioactivity".
  • 1903 – Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel) for research on radioactivity.
  • 1911 – Won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for isolating pure radium.

    • Later Achievements & Legacy

  • 1906 – Became the first female professor at the Sorbonne after Pierre’s tragic death.
  • 1914-18 – Developed mobile X-ray units for World War I battlefield hospitals.
  • 1921 – Received 1 gram of radium from U.S. President Harding for her research.
  • 1934 – Died on July 4 from radiation-induced leukemia.
  • 1995 – Interred in the Panthéon in Paris, the first woman honored for her own achievements.

"Marie Curie is, of all celebrated beings, the only one whom fame did not corrupt."
